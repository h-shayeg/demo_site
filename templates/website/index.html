Introduction

Where chemistry meets machine learning

My work sits between two disciplines that don't often share a vocabulary: computational chemistry, which is precise and physically grounded, and machine learning, which is statistical and data-hungry. I build models that respect the former while exploiting the latter — using molecular structure directly, rather than treating chemistry as an arbitrary tabular dataset.

In practice, that means graph neural networks for property prediction, generative models for candidate design, and a fair amount of unglamorous software engineering to make sure the resulting pipelines are reproducible years after a paper is published — not just on the day it's submitted.
